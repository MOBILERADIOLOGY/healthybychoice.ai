Whether you're 18 or 95, foundational wellness starts with basics. At healthybychoice.ai, we believe in simplicity first: before exploring advanced protocols, ensure your foundation is solid.

After reviewing current human research and safety profiles, these two supplements offer the broadest evidence for supporting long-term health across age groups—when used as part of a balanced lifestyle.

1. High-Quality Omega-3 (EPA/DHA)

Why include it

Omega-3 fatty acids (EPA and DHA) are essential fats the body can't make on its own. Human studies associate adequate intake with support for cardiovascular function, cognitive health, and healthy inflammation response—key pillars of longevity at any age.

2. Vitamin D3 + K2 (Cholecalciferol + MK-7)

Why include it

Vitamin D supports immune function, bone mineralization, and cellular health. Many people have suboptimal levels, especially with limited sun exposure. Adding K2 (as MK-7) helps direct calcium to bones and teeth—supporting the synergy between these nutrients.

My take

"I don't guess on dosage. I prefer getting levels tested first, then supplementing accordingly. For general maintenance, 1000–2000 IU D3 with 90–120mcg K2 (MK-7) is a common research-backed range—but your needs may differ."
